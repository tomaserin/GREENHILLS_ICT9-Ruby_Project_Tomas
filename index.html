How to Prevent Fires in Your Home

This month is Fire Protection month! Here are simple yet effective steps you could do to keep you and the people around you safe.

Unplug Unused Electronics

Whenever there is too much electricity running through something in your house, there is a high risk of a fire. You can reduce your chances of a fire starting by unplugging these appliances while you're not using them.

Use Surge Protectors

Ensure that all of your electronics are plugged into surge protectors. The most likely time for an electrical fire is during a burst in power, so by putting devices into a surge protector, you can stop worrying about the extra voltage getting to the object and starting a fire. The surge protector stops that excess from entering and can significantly lower your chance of starting a fire or its intensity if you do.

Keep Flammable Materials From Away

Fabrics, papers, etc are example of flammable materials. How do you know if something is flammable? There may be a warning or an icon that says 'Flammable'.

Avoid Smoking Indoors

Cigarettes are an easy gateway to a fire breakout. There are designated areas for smoking; if you have a deck/balcony it would be best to smoke outside rather inside your home.
